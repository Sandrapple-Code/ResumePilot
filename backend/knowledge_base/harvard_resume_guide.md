# Harvard Resume Guide

This document summarizes the core principles of resume writing according to Harvard Career Services guidelines.

## Formatting Rules
- **Length**: Keep your resume strictly to a single page for most undergraduate and early-career candidates.
- **Margins**: Margins should be between 0.5 inches and 1.0 inch on all sides.
- **Font**: Use clean, professional fonts such as Times New Roman, Arial, or Calibri, with font sizes between 10pt and 12pt for body text.
- **Hierarchy**: Use bolding and capitalization consistently to demarcate sections (Education, Experience, Projects).
- **Structure**: Organize entries in reverse chronological order within each section, showing your most recent role first.

## Writing Style & Impact
- **Action Verbs**: Always start every description bullet point with a strong action verb (e.g., *Directed*, *Analyzed*, *Engineered*, *Optimized*). Avoid passive phrasing like "responsible for" or "helped to".
- **Result-Oriented Bullets**: Focus on accomplishments rather than tasks. Describe what you did, how you did it, and the results achieved.
